My name is Will Bodeau, and I'm an incoming PhD student at UC San Diego's Halıcıoğlu Data Science Institute. My research interests sit at the intersection of spatial statistics and machine learning, with  applications in environmental and biomedical systems. I'm drawn to how the same statistical challenges recur across spatially dependent systems, from remote sensing to biomedical imaging. For example, spatial methods originally developed to tackle problems in climate science can be adapted to biomedical data, preserving spatial structure in contexts where it is often overlooked.

I received my B.S. in Statistics and Data Science from UCLA, with a minor in Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences. At UCLA I investigated antimicrobial resistance in environmental *E. coli* and human subjectivity in single-cell RNA-seq annotation. I was also an intern at NASA JPL, where I worked on space-based coastal flood detection by integrating SWOT satellite altimetry and tide gauge observations. This summer I am returning to the Sea Level and Ice group at JPL to develop open-source Julia tools for large-scale Earth observing analytics.
